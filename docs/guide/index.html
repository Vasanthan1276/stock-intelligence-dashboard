How to Read V Stock Intelligence
A plain-language guide to the terminology, scores and opportunity screens used throughout the dashboard.
Dashboard Rating
Ratings such as Strong Buy, Buy, Hold, Watch and Caution are automated quantitative ratings created by this dashboard. They are not analyst recommendations and should be used as a starting point for further research.
Overall Score Methodology
Technical Score25%
Momentum Score20%
Fundamental Score40%
Valuation Score15%
Core Investment Scores
Technical Score
Measures current price trends using moving averages, RSI and MACD.
Momentum Score
Measures recent price performance over several periods including one week, one month, three months and six months.
Fundamental Score
Evaluates available business information such as revenue growth, earnings growth, margins, return on equity, debt and liquidity.
Valuation Score
Estimates how attractively a stock appears priced using available valuation metrics such as forward P/E and price-to-book.
Data Quality
Shows how much of the fundamental scoring model had usable data. Lower data quality means the score should be interpreted with more caution.
Market Opportunity Screens
Oversold Watch
Stocks with RSI below 35. This indicates weak recent price momentum but does not guarantee a rebound.
Momentum Leaders
Stocks with strong recent price performance that also meet minimum fundamental and data-quality requirements.
Value Opportunities
Combines the Valuation Score and Fundamental Score equally.
Best Balanced
Looks for stocks with more consistent scores across Technical, Momentum, Fundamentals and Valuation.
Technical Terms
RSI — Relative Strength Index
Below 30: heavily oversold
30–40: weak
40–60: broadly neutral
60–70: strong momentum
Above 70: potentially overbought
Moving Average
The average share price over a specified number of trading days. The dashboard uses 20-, 50- and 200-day averages.
MACD
A trend and momentum indicator used as one component of the Technical Score.
Important Limitations
The dashboard uses automated third-party market and financial data. Data may occasionally be delayed, incomplete or inconsistent. Quantitative scores cannot fully capture company-specific risks, management quality, macroeconomic conditions or sudden market changes.
